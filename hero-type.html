Why the Letter G

Character selection and significance

I chose G because it's the first letter of my name, Gurmann. Beyond the personal connection, G has this perfect balance of curves and hard edges that makes it ideal for examining how typography can mimic tattoo aesthetics.

The circular bowl feels organic, almost like it was carved freehand. The crossbar cuts through the counter with precision, it's structured but not rigid. That tension between flow and control is exactly what drew me to polka tattoo style in the first place.

Typographic Details

Anatomy and structure

  • Upper Aperture: The opening at the top is sharp and deliberate, like the entry point of a tattoo needle.
  • Crossbar: Carved into the counter, it creates this diagonal tension that echoes the grid system on the homepage.
  • Ink Taper: The serif terminals fade gracefully, mimicking how ink settles and spreads on skin.

Design Approach

How form meets function

I scaled the G up to make it feel like you're examining a tattoo stencil under a magnifying glass. The monochrome gradient keeps the focus on the letterform's structure rather than adding color distractions.

The annotation lines are minimal, just thin rules that point out key anatomical features. They work like the guidelines tattoo artists use to map out placement and pressure points on skin.

What I wanted to show here is that a single character can carry the same visual weight as a full tattoo composition. The G isn't just a letter, it's a specimen, a design object with its own geometry and rhythm.
